# Website Build Overview

## Purpose

This document defines the purpose, scope, objectives, architecture philosophy, SEO philosophy, schema philosophy, governance, and strategic intent of the Velu website.

The website is the firm's primary public digital presence. It should communicate Velu's nonprofit specialization, demonstrate expertise in nonprofit accounting and advisory services, educate nonprofit leaders, generate qualified opportunities, and support long-term authority building around nonprofit financial management.

## Vision

The Velu website exists to:

- Serve as the firm's primary digital presence.
- Clearly communicate Velu's nonprofit specialization.
- Demonstrate expertise in nonprofit accounting and advisory services.
- Educate nonprofit leaders.
- Generate qualified opportunities.
- Support long-term authority building around nonprofit financial management.

The website should help nonprofit leaders understand how stronger financial information, better reporting, clearer program-finance connection, responsible funding management, and advisory support contribute to sustainable growth and stronger mission stewardship.

## Website Positioning Philosophy

Velu is positioned as a nonprofit accounting and advisory firm.

The website should reinforce expertise in:

- Nonprofit bookkeeping
- Nonprofit accounting
- Outsourced accounting
- Client advisory services
- Grant advisory and compliance
- Financial reporting
- Budgeting
- Forecasting
- Financial leadership

The website should not position Velu as a general CPA firm. Copy, page structure, schema, imagery, and conversion paths should reinforce nonprofit specialization and avoid generic small-business accounting language when a nonprofit-specific framing is more accurate.

Website messaging should align with Velu's service outcome framework:

1. Financial Foundation
2. Program-Finance Connection
3. Funding Confidence
4. Sustainable Growth

Services should be presented as the operating support that creates these outcomes. The website should not lead with task lists in isolation when the stronger message is leadership clarity, funding confidence, decision support, and financial stewardship.

## Architecture Philosophy

The website should be structured around clear user intent and service authority.

The homepage should establish Velu's nonprofit specialization, introduce the firm's outcome framework, connect those outcomes to core services, and direct qualified visitors toward the primary conversion action.

Service pages should each focus on a distinct nonprofit financial need. Current known service pages include:

- Outsourced Accounting Services
- Client Advisory Services

Future service and resource pages should support the same architecture by strengthening topical authority around nonprofit accounting, advisory, grant compliance, budgeting, forecasting, reporting, and financial leadership.

Site structure should remain understandable to nonprofit leaders and search engines. Navigation labels, headings, page URLs, internal links, schema, and calls to action should reinforce the same content model.

## SEO Philosophy

Velu's SEO strategy should be built around long-term authority in nonprofit financial management.

The website should use:

- Topic cluster approach
- Service-page authority
- Educational content
- Search intent alignment
- Long-term authority building
- Nonprofit-specific keyword targeting
- Image SEO
- Structured data

Service pages should target high-intent nonprofit accounting and advisory searches while educational resources should answer the practical questions nonprofit leaders ask before they are ready to engage. Content should be specific enough to demonstrate nonprofit expertise and broad enough to support future clusters around accounting, budgeting, grant compliance, board reporting, financial systems, and sustainable growth.

SEO implementation should not create content that is disconnected from Velu's actual expertise, services, or website experience. Search visibility should be earned through accurate positioning, useful content, clear structure, and credible signals.

## Schema Philosophy

Schema exists to help search engines understand:

- Organization
- Services
- Reviews
- Educational resources
- Geographic presence
- Conversion actions

Schema should reflect actual website content and not be used to manufacture signals. Review markup must correspond to reviews visible on the page. Offer catalogs must describe service components that are actually presented. Image markup must use actual image URLs. Lead magnet markup must describe real educational resources.

The organization schema standard uses `AccountingService` because Velu is positioned as a nonprofit accounting and advisory firm. Schema should strengthen that topical relevance while remaining accurate to the live page content.

## Image Philosophy

Website images should support nonprofit trust, clarity, and professionalism. Images should be relevant to Velu's positioning, page intent, and content hierarchy.

Image standards include:

- Use actual image URLs in schema.
- Use descriptive alt text that reflects the image and page context.
- Avoid generic imagery that weakens nonprofit specialization.
- Ensure homepage, service page, and resource imagery supports the page's search intent and conversion purpose.
- Verify published image URLs before marking schema implementation complete.

## Conversion Philosophy

The primary conversion action is:

https://velu.us/get-started

Lead magnets may be used as secondary conversion actions. Lead magnets should support education, trust building, and topic authority without distracting from the primary action for qualified prospects.

The current known lead magnet implementation is:

- The First Steps to Start a Nonprofit

Calls to action should be written for nonprofit leaders who are evaluating whether Velu can help them improve financial clarity, reporting, funding confidence, or strategic financial decision-making.

## Lead Magnet Strategy

Lead magnets should support nonprofit education and help Velu build authority around practical financial management topics.

Lead magnets may be used for:

- Early-stage nonprofit education
- Grant readiness education
- Financial foundation education
- Board and leadership reporting education
- Budgeting, forecasting, and planning education

Lead magnet pages and schema should accurately describe the resource being offered. Educational resources may use `CreativeWork` schema when the resource is real, available on the page, and aligned with the website's content strategy.
