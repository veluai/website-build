# Velu Service Outcome Framework

## Purpose

This framework organizes Velu's services around the outcomes nonprofit leaders are trying to achieve.

Nonprofit leaders are not simply looking for bookkeeping, budgeting, grant compliance, or tax services. They are seeking financial clarity, confidence, stronger decision-making, sustainable growth, and effective stewardship of funding.

This framework should guide website messaging, service pages, proposals, discovery calls, sales conversations, and future service packaging.

## The Velu Framework

Velu helps nonprofits progress through four connected financial outcomes:

1. Financial Foundation
2. Program-Finance Connection
3. Funding Confidence
4. Sustainable Growth

Together, these outcomes help nonprofit leaders understand their numbers, connect financial decisions to their mission, manage funding responsibly, and build organizations that can thrive long-term.

## Pillar 1: Financial Foundation

### Core Idea

Reliable accounting and reporting that nonprofit leaders can trust.

### What Nonprofit Leaders Want

"I want confidence that our financial information is accurate, current, and trustworthy."

### Primary Service Alignment

Outsourced Accounting

### Outcomes Created

- Reliable financial information
- Timely financial reporting
- Accessible financial information
- Strong financial infrastructure
- Reduced administrative burden
- Visibility into restricted and unrestricted funds

### Supporting Deliverables

- Bookkeeping
- Reconciliations
- Month-end close
- Financial statement preparation
- Fund accounting
- Cloud-based accounting systems
- Financial process improvements

## Pillar 2: Program-Finance Connection

### Core Idea

Helping nonprofit leaders understand the financial story behind their programs.

### What Nonprofit Leaders Want

"I want to understand how funding, staffing, expenses, and financial resources support the programs that advance our mission."

### Why It Matters

Many nonprofits can describe the impact their programs create, and many can produce financial statements. However, leaders often struggle to connect the two.

Without that connection, it becomes difficult to understand:

- Which programs are financially sustainable
- Which programs require additional funding
- How staff resources are allocated across programs
- How grant funding supports program delivery
- What programs truly cost to operate
- Where funding gaps exist
- How financial decisions affect mission outcomes

### What Success Looks Like

Organizations that successfully connect programs and finances can:

- Understand the full cost of delivering programs
- Align budgets with strategic priorities
- Track funding sources by program and service area
- Identify underfunded programs
- Make more intentional fundraising decisions
- Communicate a clearer financial story to boards and funders
- Make informed decisions about growth and sustainability

### Velu's Role

Velu helps nonprofit leaders connect programs and finances through budgeting, forecasting, board reporting, program analysis, funding analysis, and ongoing advisory services.

The goal is not simply to produce financial reports. The goal is to help leaders understand how financial resources support mission delivery and long-term sustainability.

### Primary Service Alignment

Fractional CFO & Advisory

### Outcomes Created

- Better financial decisions
- Board-ready financial reporting
- Budget development and management
- Budget vs. actual reporting
- Forecasting and scenario planning
- Program cost visibility
- Funding gap identification
- Staffing and resource allocation insights
- Strategic financial planning

### Supporting Deliverables

- Board reporting
- Dashboard reporting
- Organizational budgeting
- Program budgeting
- Budget-to-actual analysis
- Forecasting
- Cash flow planning
- Program financial analysis
- Advisory meetings

## Pillar 3: Funding Confidence

### Core Idea

Helping nonprofits secure, manage, and report on funding effectively.

### What Nonprofit Leaders Want

"I want confidence that funding is being managed responsibly, compliance requirements are being met, and future funding opportunities are being maximized."

### Primary Service Alignment

Grant Advisory & Compliance

### Outcomes Created

- Stronger grant budgets
- Better visibility into restricted funding
- Confidence managing funder requirements
- Reduced grant administration burden
- Improved funder reporting
- Stronger readiness for audits and compliance reviews
- Greater confidence pursuing future funding opportunities

### Supporting Deliverables

- Grant budget development
- Restricted funding tracking
- Grant reporting support
- Compliance monitoring
- Documentation systems
- Funding allocation support
- Grant administration guidance

## Pillar 4: Sustainable Growth

### Core Idea

The outcome created when financial foundation, program-finance connection, and funding confidence work together.

### What Nonprofit Leaders Want

"I want to build an organization that can grow responsibly, make confident decisions, and thrive long-term."

### Primary Service Alignment

All services working together

### Outcomes Created

- Stronger financial decision-making
- Greater financial sustainability
- Improved stewardship
- Increased organizational capacity
- Greater mission impact
- Reduced dependency on outside support over time

### Supporting Deliverables

- Financial infrastructure
- Leadership reporting
- Budgeting and forecasting
- Funding strategy
- Grant management
- Long-term financial planning
- Advisory support

## How This Framework Should Be Used

This framework should guide Velu's external messaging and internal service design.

### Website Messaging

Service pages should lead with outcomes before deliverables.

Examples:

- Outsourced Accounting should be positioned around building a financial foundation.
- Fractional CFO & Advisory should be positioned around connecting programs and finances.
- Grant Advisory & Compliance should be positioned around funding confidence.
- Sustainable growth should be presented as the ultimate result of the Velu approach.

### Discovery Calls

Discovery questions should explore the nonprofit leader's desired outcomes.

Examples:

- How confident are you in the accuracy and timeliness of your financial information?
- How well do you understand the financial story behind your programs?
- How confident are you managing restricted funding and grant requirements?
- What decisions would be easier if you had better financial visibility?

### Proposals

Proposals should connect service scope to desired client outcomes.

Instead of only listing tasks, proposals should explain what each service helps the organization achieve.

### Service Packaging

Future service packages should reflect the maturity journey nonprofits move through:

1. Build the financial foundation.
2. Connect programs and finances.
3. Build funding confidence.
4. Support sustainable growth.
