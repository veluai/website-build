# Website Messaging Framework

## Purpose

This document translates the Velu Service Outcome Framework into website positioning and messaging guidance.

It should serve as the primary reference for Velu's website copy, service page structure, homepage messaging, discovery call language, proposal language, sales conversations, and marketing content.

Velu's messaging should consistently prioritize the outcomes nonprofit leaders want to achieve before describing the deliverables Velu provides. Services matter because they help nonprofit leaders gain clarity, make stronger decisions, manage funding responsibly, and build financially sustainable organizations.

## 1. Firm Positioning Statement

Velu helps nonprofit leaders build the financial clarity, confidence, and infrastructure they need to make stronger decisions, manage funding responsibly, and grow sustainably.

Velu should be positioned as a nonprofit-focused finance partner, not simply as a bookkeeping, accounting, grant compliance, or tax services provider. The firm supports nonprofit leaders across a connected financial maturity journey:

1. Build a reliable financial foundation.
2. Connect programs and finances.
3. Strengthen funding confidence.
4. Support sustainable growth.

Website messaging should make clear that Velu understands the specific financial realities of nonprofit organizations, including restricted funding, board reporting, funder requirements, program costs, cash flow pressure, and the need to steward resources effectively.

## 2. Core Promise

Velu helps nonprofit leaders understand their numbers, connect financial decisions to mission impact, and manage funding with confidence.

The core promise should be expressed through outcomes:

- Confidence in accurate, timely financial information
- Clearer connection between programs, funding, staffing, and expenses
- Stronger budgeting, forecasting, and board reporting
- Better visibility into restricted and unrestricted funds
- Greater confidence managing grants and funder requirements
- More sustainable financial decisions over time

Velu's services should be presented as the way these outcomes are created. Deliverables such as bookkeeping, reconciliations, budget reports, grant budgets, dashboards, and advisory meetings should support the promise rather than lead the message.

## Messaging Hierarchy

Website content should generally communicate:

Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable

- Outcome = the leadership result the nonprofit seeks.
- Capability = the organizational capability required to achieve the outcome.
- Service = the Velu service that helps create the capability.
- Deliverable = the specific outputs, reports, meetings, analyses, or accounting activities provided through the service.

Example:

Funding Confidence
-> Grant Management Capability
-> Grant Advisory & Compliance
-> Grant budgets, grant reporting support, compliance monitoring, restricted fund tracking

Website copy should generally move from outcome to deliverable rather than leading with deliverables.

## 3. Financial Foundation Messaging

Financial Foundation messaging should focus on trust, reliability, clarity, and reduced administrative burden.

This outcome is for nonprofit leaders who need confidence that their financial information is accurate, current, accessible, and organized. It should speak to leaders who are tired of uncertainty, delayed reports, inconsistent processes, or limited visibility into restricted and unrestricted funds.

Primary service alignment: Outsourced Accounting.

Messaging themes:

- Reliable financial information leaders can trust
- Timely reporting that supports better decisions
- Clean accounting processes and stronger financial infrastructure
- Visibility into restricted and unrestricted funds
- Less administrative burden on internal teams

Supporting deliverables may include bookkeeping, reconciliations, month-end close, financial statement preparation, fund accounting, cloud-based accounting systems, and financial process improvements.

Service page guidance:

Lead with the outcome of a stronger financial foundation. Then explain how outsourced accounting creates that foundation through accurate books, timely close processes, fund accounting, and usable reports.

Website content should generally communicate Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable before discussing specific deliverables in detail.

## 4. Program-Finance Connection Messaging

Program-Finance Connection messaging should be treated as a differentiating concept for Velu.

Many nonprofit finance providers describe accounting, reporting, budgeting, or CFO support in operational terms. Velu should frame this work around helping leaders understand the financial story behind their programs: how funding, staffing, expenses, and financial resources support the mission.

This concept should help Velu stand apart by showing that nonprofit financial management is not only about producing reports. It is about connecting financial information to program strategy, leadership decisions, board conversations, and mission impact.

Primary service alignment: Fractional CFO & Advisory.

Messaging themes:

- Understand what programs really cost
- Connect budgets to program strategy and mission priorities
- Identify funding gaps before they become urgent
- Give leaders and boards clearer financial visibility
- Use forecasting and scenario planning to support confident decisions
- Translate financial reports into practical leadership insight

Supporting deliverables may include board reporting, dashboard reporting, organizational budgeting, program budgeting, budget-to-actual analysis, forecasting, cash flow planning, program financial analysis, and advisory meetings.

Service page guidance:

Lead with the outcome of stronger program-finance connection. Then explain how fractional CFO and advisory support helps leaders use budgets, reports, forecasts, and analysis to understand the financial reality behind their programs.

Website content should generally communicate Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable before discussing specific deliverables in detail.

## 5. Funding Confidence Messaging

Funding Confidence messaging should focus on responsible funding management, restricted fund visibility, compliance readiness, and stronger funder reporting.

This outcome is for nonprofit leaders who need confidence that grants and restricted funds are being managed properly, funder requirements are being met, and future funding opportunities can be pursued from a stronger financial position.

Primary service alignment: Grant Advisory & Compliance.

Messaging themes:

- Stronger grant budgets and funding plans
- Better visibility into restricted funding
- Confidence managing funder requirements
- Clearer documentation and reporting systems
- Reduced grant administration burden
- Better readiness for audits and compliance reviews
- More confidence pursuing future funding opportunities

Supporting deliverables may include grant budget development, restricted funding tracking, grant reporting support, compliance monitoring, documentation systems, funding allocation support, and grant administration guidance.

Service page guidance:

Lead with the outcome of funding confidence. Then explain how grant advisory and compliance support helps nonprofits manage restricted funding, meet funder expectations, and strengthen future funding readiness.

Website content should generally communicate Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable before discussing specific deliverables in detail.

## 6. Sustainable Growth Messaging

Sustainable Growth messaging should present the combined result of Velu's approach.

Sustainable growth is the outcome created when a nonprofit has a reliable financial foundation, a clear connection between programs and finances, and confidence managing funding. It should be positioned as the long-term benefit of working with Velu across services.

Primary service alignment: All services working together.

Messaging themes:

- Stronger financial decision-making
- Greater financial sustainability
- Improved stewardship of resources
- Increased organizational capacity
- Greater mission impact
- More confident long-term planning

Supporting deliverables may include financial infrastructure, leadership reporting, budgeting and forecasting, funding strategy, grant management, long-term financial planning, and advisory support.

Service page and homepage guidance:

Use sustainable growth as the ultimate result of Velu's work. Avoid treating growth as expansion for its own sake. For nonprofit audiences, growth should mean responsible capacity building, stronger stewardship, healthier decision-making, and deeper mission impact.

## 7. Homepage Messaging Hierarchy

The homepage should move from the leader's desired outcome to Velu's service model.

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Lead with Velu's firm positioning statement.
2. Name the nonprofit leader's core challenge: financial information, program decisions, and funding requirements often feel disconnected.
3. Introduce Velu's core promise: clarity, confidence, and connected financial decision-making.
4. Present the four outcome pillars: Financial Foundation, Program-Finance Connection, Funding Confidence, and Sustainable Growth.
5. Show how Velu's services support those outcomes: Outsourced Accounting, Fractional CFO & Advisory, and Grant Advisory & Compliance.
6. Reinforce nonprofit focus through language about restricted funding, board reporting, program costs, funder requirements, stewardship, and mission impact.
7. Invite the next step through a discovery call or consultation focused on the organization's financial goals.

Homepage copy should not open by listing services in isolation. The page should first establish what nonprofit leaders are trying to achieve, then show how Velu helps them get there.

## 8. Service Page Messaging Rules

Service pages should lead with outcomes and support them with deliverables.

Website content should generally communicate Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable before discussing specific deliverables in detail.

Messaging rules:

- Start each service page with the leadership outcome the service creates.
- Use nonprofit-specific language throughout the page.
- Explain how the service helps leaders make decisions, manage funding, or strengthen financial infrastructure.
- Introduce deliverables only after the outcome is clear.
- Connect each deliverable to a practical benefit for nonprofit leaders.
- Avoid presenting Velu as a generic accounting or consulting firm.
- Avoid leading with task lists such as bookkeeping, reconciliations, dashboards, or grant reports without explaining why they matter.
- Use Program-Finance Connection as a recurring concept where advisory, budgeting, forecasting, board reporting, and program analysis are discussed.
- Reinforce that services are connected, not isolated. Outsourced Accounting supports reliable information, advisory turns that information into decisions, and grant advisory helps manage funding responsibly.

Recommended service page pattern:

1. Outcome-focused headline
2. Nonprofit leader challenge
3. How Velu helps
4. Outcomes created
5. Supporting deliverables
6. When this service is the right fit
7. Related services or next step

## 9. Discovery Call Messaging Guidance

Discovery call language should explore outcomes before scope.

The goal is to understand what the nonprofit leader is trying to improve, not simply which tasks they want outsourced. Questions should connect financial work to leadership confidence, decision-making, funding responsibility, and sustainability.

Discovery themes and sample questions:

- Financial Foundation: How confident are you in the accuracy and timeliness of your financial information?
- Financial Foundation: Where do your current accounting or reporting processes create the most burden?
- Program-Finance Connection: How well do you understand the financial story behind your programs?
- Program-Finance Connection: What program, staffing, or funding decisions would be easier with better financial visibility?
- Funding Confidence: How confident are you managing restricted funding and grant requirements?
- Funding Confidence: Where do funder reporting, documentation, or compliance requirements create pressure?
- Sustainable Growth: What financial decisions feel hardest to make with your current information?
- Sustainable Growth: What would responsible growth look like for your organization over the next few years?

Discovery calls should use service language after the desired outcomes are understood. Once the leader's goals are clear, Velu can connect those goals to the appropriate service mix:

- Outsourced Accounting for reliable financial foundation
- Fractional CFO & Advisory for program-finance connection and leadership decision support
- Grant Advisory & Compliance for funding confidence
- Integrated service support for sustainable growth
