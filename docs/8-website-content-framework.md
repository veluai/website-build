# Website Content Framework

## Purpose

This document defines what content belongs on the Velu website, what content does not belong on the website, and how future content should support the firm's nonprofit accounting and advisory positioning.

The framework exists to help future website work stay focused on public-facing strategy, qualified prospect education, authority building, and conversion.

## Scope

This framework applies to public website content, including core pages, service pages, educational resources, lead magnets, future articles, resource libraries, and topic clusters.

It does not govern internal SOPs, client delivery documentation, private knowledge bases, CRM workflows, client portals, or internal operating records.

## Website Content Philosophy

The website exists to:

- Educate nonprofit leaders
- Demonstrate expertise
- Build trust
- Generate qualified opportunities
- Support nonprofit financial decision-making

The website does not exist to:

- Store internal knowledge
- Publish SOPs
- Function as a client portal
- Serve as a document repository

Website content should help nonprofit leaders understand their financial challenges, evaluate Velu's expertise, and take a clear next step when Velu is a fit.

## Content Categories

### Core Pages

Examples:

- Homepage
- Service pages
- About page
- Contact page

Core pages should establish firm identity, trust, service relevance, and conversion paths.

### Service Pages

Examples:

- Outsourced Accounting
- Client Advisory Services
- Grant Advisory & Compliance

Service pages should connect the Velu Service Outcome Framework to specific public services. They should generally communicate Outcome -> Capability -> Service -> Deliverable before discussing detailed deliverables.

### Educational Resources

Examples:

- Lead magnets
- Guides
- Resource pages

Educational resources should answer practical nonprofit financial management questions and support authority building. They should connect to relevant services and conversion paths when appropriate.

### Future Thought Leadership

Examples:

- Articles
- Resource libraries
- Topic clusters

Future thought leadership should build topical depth around approved nonprofit financial management clusters. It should not drift into generic CPA, generic tax, or unrelated business content.

## Content Approval Standard

Content should generally satisfy at least one of:

- Supports a service
- Supports a topic cluster
- Supports nonprofit authority
- Supports conversion

Content that does not satisfy one of these criteria should not be added to the public website without a documented strategic reason and approval.

## Content Exclusions

Examples of excluded content:

- Generic CPA content
- Generic tax content
- Industry content unrelated to nonprofit financial management
- Internal operations content
- Proprietary frameworks not intended for public use

Excluded content may still belong in internal systems or private documentation, but it should not be published on the public website unless approved for public use.

## Content Lifecycle

Website content should move through the following lifecycle:

Proposed -> Planned -> Draft -> Published -> Revised -> Archived

Lifecycle status should be clear when future content is discussed. Proposed content is not approved work. Planned content has strategic approval but may still require copy, design, schema, image, and validation work before publication.

## Future AI Agent Instructions

- Avoid creating content without strategic purpose.
- Avoid generic accounting content.
- Reinforce nonprofit specialization.
- Align content with the Service Outcome Framework.
- Align content with approved topic clusters.
- Distinguish public website content from internal knowledge.
- Do not publish or recommend proprietary internal frameworks unless approved for public use.
