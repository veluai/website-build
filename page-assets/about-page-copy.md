## Section 1: Meet Tyler Wilcox, CPA

### Meet Tyler Wilcox, CPA

Tyler Wilcox, CPA founded Velu to help nonprofit leaders build stronger financial foundations and make more confident decisions.

With more than 15 years of accounting experience—including over a decade serving nonprofits—Tyler has supported dozens of organizations across the United States, from startups to nonprofits approaching $10 million in annual activity.

His experience spans nonprofit accounting, financial reporting, Form 990 preparation, federal tax compliance, consulting, and audit. That background shapes Velu’s approach today: financial information should be accurate and compliant, but it should also help leaders strengthen accountability and make better decisions.

Tyler is a Certified Public Accountant, a QuickBooks ProAdvisor Level 2, and holds the AICPA Nonprofit Level 1 Certificate.

## Section 2: Experience Across the Nonprofit Sector

### Experience Across the Nonprofit Sector

Each nonprofit operates within a different funding environment, program model, and set of reporting expectations. This experience helps Velu understand the broader context behind an organization’s financial information—not just the numbers themselves.

### Sector Card Grid

Create a clean visual card grid containing the following ten sectors.

#### Affordable Housing

Organizations working to expand housing access, strengthen communities, and manage complex development and funding structures.

#### Workforce Development

Programs supporting job readiness, training, employment opportunities, and long-term economic mobility.

#### Youth and Family Services

Organizations providing direct support, education, advocacy, and community programs for children, youth, and families.

#### Human Services

Mission-driven organizations addressing essential needs, community wellbeing, and access to critical services.

#### Food Insecurity

Programs focused on hunger relief, food access, nutrition, distribution, and community support.

#### Sustainable and Regenerative Agriculture

Organizations advancing responsible agriculture, land stewardship, resilient food systems, and environmental sustainability.

#### Rural and Economic Development

Organizations supporting local economies, community investment, entrepreneurship, and sustainable regional growth.

#### Education and Schools

Nonprofits and educational organizations serving students, schools, educators, and learning initiatives.

#### Arts and Culture

Organizations supporting creative expression, cultural preservation, public programming, and community enrichment.

#### Membership Associations and 501(c)(6) Organizations

Associations serving members, industries, professions, and shared economic or community interests.

### Implementation Notes

* Use a clean ten-card grid.
* Each card should include:
  * Sector title
  * Small, consistent icon or illustration
  * One concise explanatory sentence
* Keep the cards visually consistent.
* Avoid long descriptions.
* The card grid should be easy to scan on desktop and stack cleanly on mobile.
* The recommended desktop layout is five cards per row if the available width supports it; otherwise use a balanced three- or four-column layout.
